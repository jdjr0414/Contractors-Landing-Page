## What are construction business loans?

Construction business loans are term loans designed for contractors and construction companies. They can fund expansion, equipment purchases, acquisitions, working capital, or other business needs. Terms and structure vary by lender and use. Unlike a line of credit, a business loan typically provides a lump sum with a fixed repayment schedule. This structure can work well when the need is defined and the repayment can be planned.

## How do construction business loans differ from working capital?

Business loans are typically term loans with fixed repayment schedules. [Contractor working capital](/contractor-working-capital) products are often shorter-term and designed for immediate operating needs like payroll or materials. Business loans may suit larger, longer-term needs such as expansion or acquisition. Working capital fits when the need is short-term and tied to cash flow timing.

## When should contractors consider business loans?

Contractors may consider business loans when they need capital for expansion, equipment purchases, acquisition of another company, commercial real estate, or larger working capital needs. If the amount is significant and the use is defined, a term loan can provide structure and predictability. SBA loans, conventional bank loans, and alternative lenders all offer options for construction businesses.

## Can contractors get SBA loans for construction?

Yes. SBA loans are available to qualifying construction businesses for purposes such as equipment, real estate, working capital, and acquisition. SBA 7(a) and 504 programs are commonly used by contractors. Qualification requirements and terms differ from conventional loans. SBA loans often offer longer terms and lower down payments for real estate and equipment.

## Business loans vs other funding options

For short-term payroll or material gaps, [contractor working capital](/contractor-working-capital) or [contractor payroll funding](/contractor-payroll-funding) may be more appropriate. For equipment, [construction equipment financing](/construction-equipment-financing) is often structured around the asset. For flexible recurring access, a [contractor line of credit](/contractor-line-of-credit) may fit. Business loans are for defined, longer-term needs.

## What lenders typically review

Lenders typically review revenue history, profitability, time in business, credit history, and the purpose of funds. Documentation requirements vary. SBA loans may require more paperwork than some alternative products. Understanding what you need and what you can provide helps narrow the options.
