## Bond capacity is too low for the job

Many projects require **bid, performance, and payment bonds** up to the contract amount. If your [bond capacity](/contractor-bonding-and-financing) is below the job size, you can’t bid—or you bid and lose to someone who can get bonded. That’s why [contractors lose projects because of bonding](/why-contractors-lose-projects-bonding). Sureties set your capacity based on **working capital**, liquidity, credit, and track record. When those are weak, see [why contractors can't secure bonding](/why-contractors-cant-secure-bonding). **Fix**: [Contractor bonding and financing](/contractor-bonding-and-financing)—using [contractor working capital](/contractor-working-capital) or a [contractor line of credit](/contractor-line-of-credit) to strengthen your balance sheet—can help sureties increase your limit so you can compete for larger work. For how bids get rejected, see [why contractor bids get rejected](/why-contractor-bids-get-rejected).

## Can't get bonded at all

Some contractors **can’t get bonded** yet. That disqualifies them from most public and many large commercial jobs. The reasons are usually the same as [why contractors can’t secure bonding](/why-contractors-cant-secure-bonding): insufficient working capital, weak revenue or credit, or short history. **Path**: Build [contractor working capital](/contractor-working-capital) and liquidity; clean up credit; keep financials current. [Contractor bonding and financing](/contractor-bonding-and-financing) is designed for this—financing that supports your ability to get bonded. Once you’re bondable, you stop [losing projects because of bonding](/why-contractors-lose-projects-bonding) at the “can’t get bonded” level. For government work barriers, see [what stops contractors from bidding on government contracts](/what-stops-contractors-bidding-government-contracts).

## Timing and bid deadlines

Sometimes you **could** get bonded for the job, but the surety process takes too long and you miss the bid deadline. That’s another way [contractors lose projects because of bonding](/why-contractors-lose-projects-bonding). **Mitigation**: Build your bonding relationship and capacity **before** you need it. Have [contractor working capital](/contractor-working-capital) or a [contractor line of credit](/contractor-line-of-credit) in place so your financials look strong when you approach the surety. That can speed approval and help you meet deadlines. For capacity and scaling, see [why contractors can't take on bigger jobs](/why-contractors-cant-take-bigger-jobs) and [what keeps contractors from scaling](/what-keeps-contractors-from-scaling).

## What to do so you stop losing work over bonding

**Short term**: Get clear feedback from your surety or agent on why you were denied or limited. **Build**: Add [contractor working capital](/contractor-working-capital) or a [contractor line of credit](/contractor-line-of-credit) so your balance sheet and liquidity improve. **Reapply** for bonding once you’re stronger.
