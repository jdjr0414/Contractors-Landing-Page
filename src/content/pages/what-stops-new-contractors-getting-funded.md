## Short time in business

Lenders prefer a **track record**. When you’re under one or two years in business, traditional banks and many SBA programs decline or limit exposure. That’s the main [thing that stops new contractors from getting funded](/what-stops-new-contractors-getting-funded). It’s not that your business is bad—it’s that there’s no history yet. **Options**: [Contractor financing for new business](/contractor-financing-new-business) and [fast contractor loans](/fast-contractor-loans) from alternative lenders often have **lower tenure requirements**. [Construction equipment financing](/construction-equipment-financing) and [contractor working capital](/contractor-working-capital) providers that work with construction may approve with less than two years. For general denial reasons, see [why contractors get denied for financing](/why-contractors-get-denied-financing).

## Limited or no revenue history

New contractors often have **thin or no** revenue on the books. Lenders want to see that you can repay. Without revenue, they’re taking a bigger risk. **What you can do**: Show **contracts or backlog**, strong personal credit, and a clear use of funds (e.g. [contractor mobilization costs](/contractor-mobilization-costs), [contractor material purchase](/contractor-material-purchase-financing), [contractor payroll](/contractor-payroll-funding)). Some lenders will fund against signed contracts or project-based cash flow. [Contractor financing for new business](/contractor-financing-new-business) and alternative [contractor working capital](/contractor-working-capital) or [contractor line of credit](/contractor-line-of-credit) products may look at these factors instead of years of tax returns. For [contractor cash flow](/contractor-cash-flow-guide) once you’re running, see [contractor cash flow problems](/contractor-cash-flow-problems).

## Credit and collateral

**Personal credit** matters when business credit doesn’t exist yet. Poor credit makes approval harder everywhere. See [contractor financing with bad credit](/contractor-financing-bad-credit) for options that may still be available. **Collateral**: New businesses often don’t have much. [Construction equipment financing](/construction-equipment-financing) uses the **equipment** as collateral, so it can be easier for new contractors to get [excavator](/excavator-financing-contractors), [skid steer](/skid-steer-financing-contractors), or [loader](/loader-financing-contractors) financing than a general loan. For why banks say no, see [why construction businesses can't get bank loans](/why-construction-businesses-cant-get-bank-loans).

## Industry risk perception

Lenders see construction as **cyclical** and project-based. New contractors are seen as higher risk. That’s why [what stops new contractors from getting funded](/what-stops-new-contractors-getting-funded) isn’t always just tenure—it’s **tenure + industry**. **Target** lenders that specialize in or regularly work with construction. They understand [contractor draw schedule](/contractor-draw-schedule-cash-flow), [contractor slow paying clients](/contractor-slow-paying-clients), and [contractor cash flow](/contractor-cash-flow-guide) and may have products and criteria that fit.
