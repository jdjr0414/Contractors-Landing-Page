## Working capital: when it fits

[Contractor working capital](/contractor-working-capital) funds **payroll**, **materials**, **mobilization**, and **short-term operating gaps**. Use it when client payments are delayed but expenses continue. For payroll specifically, see [contractor payroll funding](/contractor-payroll-funding). For materials, see [contractor material purchase financing](/contractor-material-purchase-financing). For mobilization, see [contractor mobilization costs](/contractor-mobilization-costs). For a full overview, see [contractor cash flow problems](/contractor-cash-flow-problems).

## Equipment financing: when it fits

[Construction equipment financing](/construction-equipment-financing) funds **excavators**, **skid steers**, **dump trucks**, and **machinery**. The equipment secures the financing. Payments can be structured to match the revenue the equipment generates. Use it when you need to purchase or replace equipment. For equipment-specific guides, see [excavator financing](/excavator-financing-contractors), [skid steer financing](/skid-steer-financing-contractors), and [dump truck financing](/dump-truck-financing-contractors). For repair vs replace, see [contractor equipment breakdown funding](/contractor-equipment-breakdown-funding).

## Why the distinction matters

Using **equipment financing** for payroll or materials wastes the product—you are paying for equipment financing when you need operating funds. Using **working capital** for an excavator purchase may not fit—you may need longer terms and the equipment secures equipment financing. Matching the product to the need improves fit and cost-effectiveness. For the full index, see [all funding options](/all-funding-options).

## What about equipment repair?

Equipment **repair** may be funded with [contractor working capital](/contractor-working-capital) or a [contractor line of credit](/contractor-line-of-credit)—it is an operating expense. Equipment **replacement** typically fits [construction equipment financing](/construction-equipment-financing). For the full breakdown, see [contractor equipment breakdown funding](/contractor-equipment-breakdown-funding) and our blog on [construction equipment repair emergency](/blog/construction-equipment-repair-emergency).
