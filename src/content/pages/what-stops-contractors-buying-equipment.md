## Cash is tied up in operations

Contractors need cash for [contractor payroll](/contractor-payroll-funding), [contractor material purchase](/contractor-material-purchase-financing), [contractor mobilization costs](/contractor-mobilization-costs), and [contractor cash flow](/contractor-cash-flow-guide) gaps. Writing a big check for a new [excavator](/excavator-financing-contractors) or [skid steer](/skid-steer-financing-contractors) can leave you short for the next job. That’s one of the main [things that stop contractors from buying equipment](/what-stops-contractors-buying-equipment). **Solution**: [Construction equipment financing](/construction-equipment-financing) spreads the cost over time so you keep working capital for operations. You get the machine without draining cash. For the “afford equipment” angle, see [how contractors afford heavy equipment](/how-contractors-afford-heavy-equipment) and [how contractors afford equipment for new jobs](/how-contractors-afford-equipment-new-jobs). For when repair vs replace is the issue, see [contractor equipment repair pressure](/contractor-equipment-repair-pressure) and [contractor equipment breakdown funding](/contractor-equipment-breakdown-funding).

## Loan denials and credit

When [equipment loans get denied](/why-equipment-loans-denied-construction-companies), contractors assume they can’t buy. Denials often come from banks or general-purpose lenders that don’t specialize in construction. **Equipment-focused lenders** and [construction equipment financing](/construction-equipment-financing) providers often use different criteria—they’re collateral-focused (the machine) and may work with [contractor financing with bad credit](/contractor-financing-bad-credit) situations. [Used construction equipment financing](/used-construction-equipment-financing) can be another path when new-equipment terms don’t fit. So [what stops contractors from buying equipment](/what-stops-contractors-buying-equipment) isn’t always “no one will finance”—it’s applying in the right place. For general denial reasons, see [why contractors get denied for financing](/why-contractors-get-denied-financing).

## Down payment and timing

A large down payment can be a barrier when cash is reserved for [contractor cash flow problems](/contractor-cash-flow-problems). Equipment financing can reduce the upfront amount. **Timing** also matters: if you need the machine in a hurry, [fast contractor loans](/fast-contractor-loans) and equipment lenders can sometimes fund in days to a few weeks, versus longer SBA or bank processes. For rental when you need something immediately and short-term, see [contractor equipment rental vs financing](/contractor-equipment-rental-vs-financing) and [construction equipment loans vs lease](/construction-equipment-loans-vs-lease).

## Uncertainty about which equipment to commit to

Some contractors hesitate because they’re not sure **which** machine to buy or whether the job mix will justify it. That’s a different kind of “stop”—not cash or approval, but **commitment**. [Contractor equipment rental vs financing](/contractor-equipment-rental-vs-financing) and [construction equipment loans vs lease](/construction-equipment-loans-vs-lease) can help you compare. For product-specific options, see [excavator financing](/excavator-financing-contractors), [skid steer financing](/skid-steer-financing-contractors), [loader financing](/loader-financing-contractors), and [dump truck financing](/dump-truck-financing-contractors).
