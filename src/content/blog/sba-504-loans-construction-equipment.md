SBA 504 loans can fund [construction equipment financing](/construction-equipment-financing) needs with favorable terms. This guide explains when they make sense for contractors.

## Can SBA 504 loans be used for construction equipment?

Yes. SBA 504 loans are commonly used for major equipment and real estate. They offer longer terms and lower down payments than many conventional options. For equipment-only needs, dedicated [construction equipment financing](/construction-equipment-financing) may be simpler. For SBA options, see [SBA loans for contractors](/sba-loans-for-contractors). For excavators and skid steers, see [excavator financing](/excavator-financing-contractors) and [skid steer financing](/skid-steer-financing-contractors). For operating needs, see [contractor working capital](/contractor-working-capital) and [contractor line of credit](/contractor-line-of-credit). For a full overview, see [contractor cash flow problems](/contractor-cash-flow-problems).

## When should a contractor consider SBA 504 for equipment?

When you want longer terms, can meet the documentation requirements, and may be combining equipment with real estate. SBA 504 may fit larger capital plans. For [construction business loans](/construction-business-loans) and conventional options, see our dedicated guides. For equipment without the SBA process, see [construction equipment financing](/construction-equipment-financing). For used equipment, see [used construction equipment financing](/used-construction-equipment-financing).
