New contractors face unique financing challenges. This guide explains options for [contractor working capital](/contractor-working-capital), [construction equipment financing](/construction-equipment-financing), and [contractor line of credit](/contractor-line-of-credit) when history is limited.

## What options exist for new contractors?

[Contractor working capital](/contractor-working-capital) may be available with shorter history. [Construction equipment financing](/construction-equipment-financing) uses the equipment as collateral. [SBA loans](/sba-loans-for-contractors) may fit qualifying new businesses. [Construction business loans](/construction-business-loans) vary by lender. For [contractor cash flow problems](/contractor-cash-flow-problems) and a full overview, see our dedicated guide. For payroll gaps, see [contractor payroll funding](/contractor-payroll-funding). For material timing, see [contractor material purchase financing](/contractor-material-purchase-financing).
